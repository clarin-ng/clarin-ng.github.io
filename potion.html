Potion

Find Your Perfect-fitting Cosmetics

Category

Academic concept project (team of one)

Timeline

Three weeks

Role

Research/ Visual Design/ Branding/ User Testing

Tools

Sketch/ InVision Studio/ Google Forms

Overview

The inspiration for creating Potion comes from my journey, trying to purchase cosmetic products. Potion is an app that gives consumers a streamlined experience to help them easier to find out the products they would like to purchase.  

Problem

In today’s world, there are massive choices of beauty products in the market. Sometimes consumers are overwhelmed by the vast selections. The research process to purchase the right makeup requires a lot of time and energy, and in the end, users might still end up buying the wrong products for yourself. 

  • Products don’t match their skin type or color.

  • Information or reviews are not reliable; companies might sponsor them.

  • Although “clean” products are getting popular, the term is arbitrary for consumers because every company has its standard of the definition.

Solution

Potion changes the way how the consumer buys beauty. It provides one streamlined platform to help consumers make informed purchase decisions.  With Potion, consumers can match their skin profiles with other similar users to get better insights and recommendations of the products that are suitable for them. They can also learn about the function of each ingredient to educate themselves on what to avoid. Last but not least, users can also ask questions in the forum and get advice from similar users.

Competitive Analysis

To improve the overall research and shopping experience for cosmetics, I need to dig deeper into the major companies where consumers like to shop their products. My main findings from the competitor analysis were that the majority of websites contained a lot of information about the product, however some of the info doesn’t quite help the users. The analysis allowed me to realize that there is a huge market gap that Potion can fit in to help consumers understand a product quicker and easier.



Below are some of the problems I found:

  • Most of the products are recommended for all skin types.
  • Lack of product transparency; no detail description for each ingredient.
  • Lots of reviews that may not be targeting the right skin type as the user.
  • Online shade finder frequently could not find a match for consumers.

Prototype & Testing

User Testing

User Testing I: I tested my low-fidelity wireframes with 8 users to find out whether users could successfully customize their skin profile and also learn more about a ingredient for a product.

User Testing II: 27 users (3 via Zoom and 24 via Maze, an online user testing tool) tested the final high-fidelity wireframes. Through the testings, I uncovered what users feel towards the product and also the areas to improve in the future. prototype with another 3 users.

Problems & Solutions

These usability testings provided useful feedbacks for me to improve the existing wireframes. There were three major areas to imrove:

  • The completion indicator after taking the skin test is not as apparent. The users felt not interested to go forward completing the next step.
  • The skin profile button on the account is not obvious, users take a second route (through settings) instead.
  • User found it difficult at first to look for the forum icon on the tab bar in order for them to post a question.

Major Iteration: Increase Engagement

Completing the skin profile is an important task in Potion because this can provide a data to match with the backend database to show the recommended products to users. In order to emphasis the importance, I added a progress background to boost respondent engagement.

Future Improvement #1: Easy Understand Tab Bar

Future Solution: To make the forum tab more clear to users, I will add name tag for each tab bar (like the right one) in the future.

Future Improvement #2: To view and edit skin profile with ease

Future solution: The skin profile button looks like a navigation bar title, therefore users do not think it as a CTA button. In the future, I will redesign the button and/or move it down below near the username.

Preference Testing

In order for users to quickly understand a product hightlight, I perform a perference testing on two layouts of the hightlight section with total of 26 people. They were ask on two questions:


1) Imagine if you are looking for a non-creulty product, which layout do you think it's easier to determine if a product is not cruelty free?


2) Which layout do you gernerally prefer?

Result

Majority (70% for question 1 and 75% for question 2) of the respondents prefer option A for both questions. They mentioned that a list is easier and faster to scan compare to option B, which creates distractions. Therefore I decided to move on with option A

Conclusion-
what I learned

Potion is a project that I always dream of creating. While I was so passionate and eager to make it happen, sometimes I added in my own bias. I learned that it's essential to be reminded about the user's needs so that it could help me justify my design with confidence. Performing usability testing and preference testing also helps me to confirm a lot of uncertainties.


Overall, I believe this product is successful; it has fulfilled the users' goals and solves their problem. According to the usability testings, the majority of the respondents responded that the ingredients list was helpful, and the app will make their research process easier. However, there were a few who were skeptical about the idea of this app. They mentioned that they would use it unless the product has trustable, non-sponsored reviews, and also the products that they would like to see. From a business standpoint, I agree with their point of view. Potion requires a lot of database and development effort to be successful. The ultimate goal for this app is to provide transparency and unbias data to the consumers. I would like to see how this app will be integrated with the real database.
